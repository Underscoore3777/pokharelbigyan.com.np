Phylum Coelenterata

(Koilas + Enteron)

Introduction

  • Aquatic animals, mostly marine; some freshwater
  • Diploblastic → body made of two layers
  • Radial symmetry → parts arranged around central axis

Body Structure

  • Body wall: Epidermis, Gastrodermis, and Mesoglea
  • Single cavity: Coelenteron
  • Mouth is the only opening
  • Tentacles help in locomotion & food capture

Specialized Cells

  • Cnidocytes → stinging cells for defense & prey
  • Nerve cells → simple nerve net
  • Epitheliomuscular cells → contraction
  • Interstitial cells → regeneration

Key Features

  • Tissue-level organization
  • Two body forms:
    • Polyp → sessile, cylindrical
    • Medusa → free-swimming
  • Respiration through body surface

Subphyla

  • Anthozoa → only polyp stage (Corals)
  • Medusozoa → polyp + medusa stages (Jellyfish, Hydra)

Classes

  • Hydrozoa → Hydra, Obelia
  • Scyphozoa → True jellyfish (Aurelia)
  • Anthozoa → Corals, Sea anemones
  • Cubozoa → Box jellyfish

Reproduction

  • Asexual → budding and regeneration
  • Sexual → gametes → planula larva
  • Alternation of generations: Polyp ↔ Medusa

Importance

  • Coral reefs support biodiversity
  • Vital part of the marine food chain
  • Jellyfish stings can be harmful
  • Ecological and aesthetic value
